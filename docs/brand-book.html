01 — Brand Essence

The soul of the brand

Mademoiselle in the Kitchen is an artisan French pâtisserie, made to order — French homemade treats made with love, baked fresh for your pickup date, and delivered with warmth and care.

🥐

Authentically French

The recipes draw from French tradition — madeleines, canelés, sablés, meringues, and more. They are inspired by that tradition, sometimes adapted to welcome everyone around the table, including the little ones. Honest, flavourful, and never Americanized in spirit.

✦

Made to Order, Always Fresh

Nothing sits on a shelf. Every order is baked fresh and ready for your pickup date. This is the opposite of mass production — it is handmade food that someone actually thought about, prepared for you specifically.

💛

The Goûter Spirit

Born from the French after-school snack tradition — the beloved goûter — this brand carries the warmth of a home kitchen. An everyday ritual of care, shared with family, neighbors, and anyone lucky enough to get a box.

🌺

A Warm, Personal Experience

Every order is a conversation, not a transaction. You reach out, we bake for you. It is personal, attentive, and rooted in genuine hospitality — the kind that makes you feel welcome before you've even taken a bite.

✦

Mission

To share a little taste of France with every home in Miami — one handmade treat at a time, made fresh for the people who order it.

Vision

To become the most beloved French cottage pâtisserie in South Florida — a name that means quality, care, and a guaranteed smile.

Values

Authenticity · Savoir-faire · Generosity · Warmth · Joy

02 — Brand Story

The story behind the apron

In her own words

"A home bakery born in French tradition, made to bring a little taste of France to your table."

Ever since I was a little girl, I've loved baking — watching my mom and grandma in the kitchen was probably where it all started! Like most French kids, I began with the classic gâteau au yaourt (yogurt cake). I'd bake for my parents and their guests, but never took it much further… until I became a mom.

That's when I really got into it, baking afternoon snacks for the kids every day — the beloved French goûter tradition. It quickly became my favorite part of the day.

Then we moved to Miami, which was a wonderful adventure — but we missed the French baguette so much! I started baking it myself, tweaked the recipe to my liking, and began sharing it with neighbors and friends. When they asked if I'd sell it, and whether I made other French specialties like madeleines, I realized there was something worth pursuing here.

And so, Mademoiselle in the Kitchen was born — a home bakery born in French tradition, made to bring a little taste of France to your table. I'm always exploring new recipes, and I can't wait to share them with you!

04 — Voice & Tone

How Mademoiselle speaks

The brand voice is warm but never casual, personal but never overly familiar. It carries the charm of someone who genuinely loves baking and is happy to share it with you.

We are

Warm & personal Effortlessly chic Proudly French Honest & precise Quietly confident

We sound like

A French friend who bakes Knowing, not boastful Inviting, not salesy Storytelling, not listing Excited about craft

We are never

Generic / mass-market Overly casual or slangy Pushy or promotional Inaccurate or overpromising Apologetic or unsure

Important accuracy rules — always get these right

Say "made fresh for your pickup date" — not necessarily the same day. Baking timing varies by product.

Recipes are inspired by French tradition, not always strictly classical. Some are adapted for all audiences, including children.

Every order is personal, never mass produced — this is always true and always worth saying.

Never claim a recipe is "the authentic Bordeaux canelé" or attribute recipes to a specific family member. Keep it genuine but not overly specific.

✓ Write this way

"These pistachio chocolate madeleines are baked fresh for your pickup date — that's the only way to get the shell just right."
"The canelé is a little labour of love. Crispy on the outside, soft and custardy inside. It takes time. It's worth it."
"Merci for your order! I can't wait to bake this for you. 🥐"

✕ Avoid this

"Amazing deal! Order now and get 12 madeleines for just $15!!! 🔥🔥"
"Baked fresh the day of your pickup, using my grandmother's authentic Bordeaux recipe passed down for generations."
"Sorry for the late reply! LOL anyway here's our menu 😅"

Instagram caption formula

1. The hook

2. The story

3. The invite

07 — Logo System

The mark of Mademoiselle

The logo is a circular badge: the silhouette at center, the brand name arched around it. It is recognizable, ownable, and works at any size.

Primary — dark background

Packaging, Instagram, premium use

Light background version

Website, printed collateral, stationery

Rose profile variant

Instagram profile, stickers, gift tags

✓ Always do

  • Use Playfair Display Italic for "Mademoiselle"
  • Use Jost Light for "IN THE KITCHEN" in spaced caps
  • Keep the ✦ ornaments at 3 and 9 o'clock positions
  • Use dark version on dark backgrounds only
  • Maintain the double circle ring detail
  • Keep clear space equal to 10% of logo diameter

✕ Never do

  • Stretch, skew or rotate the logo
  • Place on a busy photographic background
  • Use colors outside the brand palette
  • Add drop shadows or glow effects
  • Remove the silhouette from the badge
  • Use the logo at less than 40px diameter

08 — Photography & Visual Direction

Images that make you taste

Photography is the brand's most powerful tool on Instagram. Every image should feel like it was taken in a sun-filled kitchen on a Saturday morning.

01

Natural light, always

Shoot near a window, in soft morning or afternoon light. No harsh flash. The warmth of natural light is the warmth of the brand.

02

Warm, muted tones

Edit toward warm creams, soft pinks, and golden tones. Avoid cool blue filters or heavy saturation. Think butter, linen, honey.

03

Real & honest moments

A slight dusting of powdered sugar on the table. A madeleine cooling on a rack. Real moments from a real kitchen. Slight imperfection feels warm and trustworthy.

04

Generous negative space

Don't crowd the frame. Let one beautiful canelé sit alone on a cream linen cloth. White space signals care. Cluttered frames signal market stall.

✕ Photography to avoid

  • Moody dark backgrounds
  • Cold blue or green tones
  • Heavy filters or oversaturation
  • Text overlays on product photos
  • Cluttered flat lays
  • Stock photography
  • Artificial backgrounds
  • Worm's-eye product angles
